Namaste and Welcome to my Website!!
I am currently a senior at Smithtown High School West, in Smithtown, Long Island. I have been studying music since I was four years old and hope to continue my musical journey in the years to come.

About me:
I was born on October 23, 1996 in Dubai, United Arab Emirates. Currently, I am an 11th grader at Smithtown High School West in Smithtown, Long Island. My passion for music was recognized during my early childhood by my parents, Anup and Manisha Varshney. I started my musical training at the tender age of four under local teachers and musicians. After relocating to the United States, in 2004, I joined the Raga School of Music in Hicksville, New York. There, I started my training in North Indian classical singing under Bhajan Samrat Shri Kumar Raj Gandharav. Under the guidance and patronage of my Guru, I have recorded my debut Bhajan album. I dedicate this album, “Bhajan Prabhat”, to my grandfather, late Shri Prabhat Kumar Varshney. I have performed in many concerts at the Shri Ram Sharnam Temple, the Hindu Community Center and Shri Hanuman Mandir. In addition to singing, I am a fine violinist and lead my school's orchestral performances as concert mistress. Presently, I am learning under Lisa Polito. I have participated in the Long Island String Festival Association(LISFA) and Suffolk County Music Educator’s Association(SCMEA) several times. I have also learnt Bharatnatyam, an Indian classical dance, under Guru Smt. Satya Pradeep.

My Performances:
On October 30, 2010, I released my debut Bhajan album, “Bhajan Prabhat”, in honor of my grandfather, late Shri Prabhat Kumar Varshney. To commemorate my achievement, I performed live at my Bhajan Sandhya. At my Bhajan Sandhya, I sang some of my favorite Bhajans, which was followed by a CD releasing ceremony. In addition, my Guru, Bhajan Samrat Shri Kumar Raj Gandharav, sang some Bhajans as well. To conclude my concert, I performed a fusion of “Sare Jahan Se Aacha” and "Jana Gana Mana," the national anthem of India, on my violin and then sang the Aarti.
